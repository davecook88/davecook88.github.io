About me

From Britain but living in Querétaro, Mexico since April 2017. I moved here after finishing my masters degree in Teaching English as a Second or Other Language (TESOL). Over the past couple of years, I've been working on some programming projects through work and in my own time.

I've made this page as a way to share the lessons I've learned in programming and web design as well as in teaching and learning languages. If you would be interested in working or collaborating with me, I'd love to hear from you!

Language Learning

I've always been fascinate by languages. At school I started with French and German and later started studying Spanish. When deciding what to do at university, languages seemed like the obvious option.

As a result of language learning, I have had the opportunity to live and work in in Spain, France, Germany, Colombia, Japan and now Mexico. Many of the friendships and experiences that I had would not have been possible if I had only spoken English.

While living in Japan, I studied Japanese, my greatest challenge so far. I managed to pass the JLPT N2 test after one year's study.

Teaching English

I've been teaching English as a foreign language for over three years. I started working at La Universidad del Norte in Barranquilla Colombia, where I spent one year. I was an ALT with the JET programme for 2 years, teaching English in Junior High Schools in Kagoshima, Japan. Currently I'm teaching mainly Business English in Queretaro, Mexico.

I have just finished the research for my masters degree in teaching English and I will share some of the things that I learned through this programme for anyone who's interested.
